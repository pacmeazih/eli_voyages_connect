ARTICLE 1 – PURPOSE OF THE CONTRACT
This contract pertains to the provision of assistance services for the Labour Market Impact Assessment (LMIA) application process with Employment and Social Development Canada (ESDC), provided by the Service Provider to the Employer, in exchange for a commission as defined in Article 7.
The services provided include, but are not limited to:
•	Assessment of the Employer's and candidate's eligibility for the LMIA program;
•	Assistance in preparing the LMIA application file (detailed job offer, company documents, recruitment evidence, candidate documents: ${candidate_name} for the position of ${job_position});
•	Verification of compliance with all documents required by Employment and Social Development Canada (ESDC);
•	Monitoring of the file until the final decision is obtained.
The Service Provider specifies that they are neither a lawyer nor a licensed Canadian immigration consultant (RCIC), and their role is purely administrative and supportive.

ARTICLE 2 – OBLIGATIONS OF THE SERVICE PROVIDER
The Service Provider undertakes to:
•	Provide the services with diligence and professionalism;
•	Continuously inform and advise the Employer on the progress of the procedure;
•	Ensure service continuity throughout the procedure, even in the event of personnel changes.
It is specified that the Service Provider is an independent contractor with no subordinate relationship to the Employer, responsible for their own administrative and legal organization.

ARTICLE 3 – PERFORMANCE TIMELINE
The Service Provider undertakes to assist the Employer throughout the LMIA application process, for a maximum duration of ${contract_duration}, renewable by written agreement of both parties.
Processing times by ESDC generally vary between 2 and 4 months depending on the province and job category.
In the event of circumstances that may delay the performance of services (including due to the Employer), the Service Provider will inform the Employer as soon as possible with an estimate of the additional time required.

ARTICLE 4 – MONITORING OF SERVICES
The Service Provider undertakes to regularly monitor the progress of the procedures through phone calls or electronic exchanges every two months, or at another frequency agreed upon by the parties.

ARTICLE 5 – ACCEPTANCE OF SERVICES
The Service Provider will inform the Employer upon completion of each significant stage of the process. The acceptance of services will involve a compliance check and validation by the Employer.

ARTICLE 6 – OBLIGATIONS OF THE EMPLOYER
The Employer undertakes to:
•	Provide all necessary documents and information in a timely manner for the proper execution of the service (detailed job offer, company documents, recruitment evidence compliant with ESDC requirements, foreign candidate's documents);
•	Comply with Canadian employment standards (median wage, working conditions);
•	Actively collaborate with the Service Provider and inform them of any difficulties or changes in circumstances;
•	Pay the fees and charges as per the agreed terms.
Failure to meet these obligations may result in delays or temporary suspension of services, for which the Service Provider shall not be held liable.

ARTICLE 9 – FORCE MAJEURE
Neither party shall be held liable for failure to fulfill contractual obligations if such failure results from a force majeure event.
Force majeure events include, but are not limited to:
•	Natural disasters, pandemics, wars, riots, or political unrest;
•	General strikes affecting public services or relevant administrations;
•	Unforeseeable changes in immigration laws or policies in the destination country;
•	Significant changes in the Employer's situation (company closure, operational bans, serious financial issues, etc.) directly impacting their ability to continue the process.
In the event of such an occurrence, the parties' obligations will be suspended for the duration of the impediment. Each party undertakes to inform the other as soon as possible.
