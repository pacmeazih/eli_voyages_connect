IT HAS BEEN AGREED AS FOLLOWS:

ARTICLE 1 – PURPOSE OF THE CONTRACT
This contract pertains to the provision of immigration assistance services for studies in Canada, provided by the Service Provider to the Client, in exchange for a commission as defined in Article 7.
The services provided include, but are not limited to:
•	Assistance with the admission process for the first cycle (university or college);
•	Searching for accommodation near the educational institution in Canada;
•	Airport reception in Canada and assistance with initial administrative procedures.
The Service Provider specifies that they are neither a lawyer nor a licensed Canadian immigration consultant (RCIC), and their role is purely administrative and supportive.

ARTICLE 2 – OBLIGATIONS OF THE SERVICE PROVIDER

The Service Provider undertakes to:

•	Provide the services with diligence and professionalism.
•	Continuously inform and advise the Client on the progress of the procedure.
•	Ensure service continuity throughout the procedure, even in the event of personnel changes.

It is specified that the Service Provider is an independent contractor with no subordinate relationship to the Client, responsible for their own administrative and legal organization.
ARTICLE 3 – PERFORMANCE TIMELINE

The Service Provider undertakes to perform the services for the academic session of …, or the following session if a postponement is necessary.	

In the event of circumstances that may delay the performance of services (including due to the Client), the Service Provider will inform the Client as soon as possible with an estimate of the additional time required.	

The initial duration of this contract is set at 12 months, renewable by written agreement of both parties.
ARTICLE 4 – MONITORING OF SERVICES

The Service Provider undertakes to regularly monitor the progress of the procedures through phone calls or electronic exchanges every two months, or at another frequency agreed upon by the parties.
ARTICLE 5 – ACCEPTANCE OF SERVICES

The Service Provider will inform the Client upon completion of each significant stage of the process. The acceptance of services will involve a compliance check and validation by the Client.




ARTICLE 6 – OBLIGATIONS OF THE CLIENT

The Client undertakes to:
•	Provide all necessary documents and information in a timely manner for the proper execution of the service;
•	Actively collaborate with the Service Provider and inform them of any difficulties or changes in circumstances;
•	Pay the fees and charges as per the agreed terms.

Failure to meet these obligations may result in delays or temporary suspension of services, for which the Service Provider shall not be held liable.

ARTICLE 7 – FEES AND CHARGES

a. Amount of Fees

The Service Provider’s fees for the services amount to 2,000,000 F CFA, excluding administrative costs.
b. Payment Terms

Payment shall be made as follows:
•	500,000 F CFA upon receipt of proof of admission.
•	1,500,000 F CFA upon submission of the study permit application (five business days after submission).

Payment must be made before the collection of biometric data, as per the Service Provider’s instructions. Any delay will automatically incur, without prior notice, the application of statutory late payment interest.

Payments may be made by bank transfer, cash deposit, or any other means agreed upon by the parties. A receipt will be systematically issued to the Client.

c. Administrative Costs

A non-refundable deposit of 500,000 F CFA per person is required upon signing the contract.
This amount covers administrative costs (translations, admission fees, documents, etc.).

A detailed statement of expenses incurred from the 500,000 F CFA deposit will be provided to the Client after the visa application submission, accompanied by supporting documents.

If part of this amount remains unused at this stage, the unspent balance will be deducted from the remaining amount owed by the Client and will appear as a credit on the final invoice.
d. Cases of Refusal or Cancellation
•	In case of visa refusal, no refund will be provided, but the Service Provider undertakes to assist the Client free of charge for the next session. Administrative costs will remain the responsibility of the Client.
•	In case of admission refusal after two attempts or cancellation by the Client before the issuance of admission, the 500,000 F CFA deposit will remain with the Service Provider.
e. Medical Examinations

Medical examinations required by Canadian authorities are the sole responsibility of the Guarantor and their beneficiaries. These must be conducted in their country of residence or at the nearest authorized center, in accordance with Immigration, Refugees and Citizenship Canada (IRCC) requirements.
ARTICLE 8 – CONFIDENTIALITY

The Service Provider undertakes to protect the confidentiality of all information and documents exchanged under this contract. No data will be disclosed to third parties without the Client’s written consent, except as required by law.




ARTICLE 9 – FORCE MAJEURE

Neither party shall be held liable for failure to fulfill contractual obligations if such failure results from a force majeure event.

Force majeure events include, but are not limited to:
•	Natural disasters, pandemics, wars, riots, or political unrest;
•	General strikes affecting public services or relevant administrations;
•	Unforeseeable changes in immigration laws or policies in the destination country;
•	Significant changes in the Client’s socio-professional situation (e.g., job loss, travel bans, serious health issues) directly impacting their ability to continue the process.

In the event of such an occurrence, the parties’ obligations will be suspended for the duration of the impediment. Each party undertakes to inform the other as soon as possible.
ARTICLE 10 – AMENDMENTS

Any amendment to this contract must be made in writing, dated, and signed by both parties.
